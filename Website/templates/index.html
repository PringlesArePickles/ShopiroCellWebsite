The CellVerse

What are cells?

Cells are the simplest life forms, with the most basic cell being called a prokaryote.

Cells evolved from bacteria eating one another

A bacteria eats another bacteria, and if they benefit eachother, the ingested bacteria gets to stay.

Eventually the bacteria (cell) engulfed enough bacteria to look like the cells we see today.

This theory is called Endosymbiosis, created by Lynn Margulis in 2001

Endosymbiotic theory led to the creation of organelles.

Organelles to a cell are like organs to humans.

History of Cell Discovery

Robert Hooke (1665):

First to observe and name "cells" after looking at cork under a microscope. Saw empty "cell" walls in plant material.

Antonie van Leeuwenhoek (1674):

Discovered "animalcules" (single-celled organisms) by observing pond water under a microscope. First to observe living cells. First to observe living cells.

Matthias Schleiden (1838):

Proposed that all plants are made of cells.

Theodor Schwann (1839):

Proposed that all animals are made of cells. Rudolf Virchow (1855): Concluded that all cells come from pre-existing cells.

Cell Theory:

1. All living things are made of cells (Schleiden & Schwann).

2. The cell is the basic unit of structure and function in living organisms.

3. All cells arise from preexisting cells (Virchow).

4. All cells contain genetically inherited material(DNA/RNA)

Plant vs. Animal Cells

Because of Endosymbiotic Theory, cells have evolved into different categories, most commonly plant and animal, each with different characteristics and functions.

Endosymbiotic theory

A long time ago, before complex cells existed, there were only simple cells—prokaryotes like bacteria.

One day, a large prokaryotic cell engulfed a smaller one, maybe a bacterium that was really good at using oxygen to make energy. But instead of digesting it, the two cells started working together. The smaller cell helped make energy, and the bigger cell gave it protection. Over time, that small cell became part of the big cell.

That’s how the mitochondria formed. Later, a similar thing happened with a cell that could do photosynthesis — that one eventually became the chloroplast in plant cells.

A Summary

Here’s a concise summary on cells:

What Are Cells?

Cells are the basic units of life in all living organisms. Every organism, from bacteria to humans, is made up of cells.

Main Cell Parts

Prokaryotic Cells

  • Found in bacteria and archaea
  • No nucleus or membrane-bound organelles
  • Smaller and simpler

Eukaryotic Cells

  • Found in plants, animals, fungi, and protists
  • Have a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles
  • Larger and more complex

Main Cell Parts

  • Cell Membrane: Controls what enters and leaves the cell
  • Cytoplasm: Jelly-like fluid where organelles float
  • Nucleus: Contains DNA; controls cell activities
  • Mitochondria: Produces energy (ATP) through respiration
  • Ribosomes: Make proteins
  • Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER): Helps in making proteins (rough ER) and lipids (smooth ER)
  • Golgi Apparatus: Packages and ships proteins
  • Lysosomes: Break down waste (mostly in animal cells)
  • Chloroplasts: Do photosynthesis (only in plant cells)
  • Cell Wall: Provides structure (only in plant cells)

Key Cell Functions

  • Reproduction: Mitosis (body cells) or meiosis (sex cells)
  • Energy Use: Cellular respiration in mitochondria
  • Protein Synthesis: DNA → RNA → Protein
  • Transport: Movement of materials across the membrane
  • Homeostasis: Maintaining a stable internal environment
